About me

For the past 30 years, I have been a PC repair technician for various companies, such as Hewlett-Packard, HelloTech, Staples, and CompUSA. This has been a rewarding and successful career until the advent of Covid. Being immunocompromised, I was ordered to stay home for two years while Covid rampaged through the world. This necessitated a career change, so I am currently enrolled in the College of Western Idaho. As a student seeking an Associate's Degree in Software Development, I am striving to generate a career that will allow me to work from home. Programming has been my hobby since the late 90s when Multi-User Domains (MUDs), text-based gaming MMORPGs, were prolific. I taught myself the C programming language by learning MUD code and ran a highly successful MUD for the next 12 years. In the following years, my self-learning has touched on HTML, CSS, Java, and MySQL.

Gaming is my passion

Be it tabletop game systems, such as Dungeons and Dragons, Cyberpunk, Shadowrun, and BattleTech amongst many others; video games on virtually every platform starting from the Atari Gaming System, all the way to the next gen consoles, such as the PlayStation 5; and strategy card games like Magic the Gathering, I thrive in the design, creation, and experience of games.

My vision

Combining my love of game theory, design, and creation with my Associate's in Software Development Degree, to produce games that people would like to play, or to be part of a team that accomplishes that goal, is my ultimate dream. To work with a company like Square-Enix or Blizzard, would be incredible beyond my wildest dreams.
